The Role of Leadership in Project Success

Leadership also plays an important role in project management. Leadership is the absolute driving force that motivates the team to overcome challenges and achieve goals. Effective project leaders are able to adjust their management style according to the project stage - providing detailed guidance at the project start-up stage, transitioning to coaching during the project execution stage, and empowering team members as the project draws to a close. For small organizations and charities, a good leader is the key force for project success.

Leadership Styles in Projects

Directive

Clear instructions and close supervision, effective in crisis situations or with inexperienced teams

Supportive

Focuses on team relationships and morale, ideal for stressful project phases

Participative

Encourages team input in decision-making, boosts innovation and ownership

Achievement-Oriented

Sets challenging goals with confidence in team capabilities, drives high performance

Case Study: Dublin Tech Startup

A software development company struggling with 40% team turnover implemented:

  • Transparent weekly CEO updates
  • Monthly one-on-one feedback sessions
  • Cross-functional decision-making teams

Within six months, they reduced turnover by 60% while accelerating product delivery timelines.

Developing Your Leadership Skills

Project leadership skills can be cultivated through deliberate practice and reflection. Consider maintaining a leadership journal to document challenges faced, decisions made, and lessons learned. Seek feedback from team members and mentors to identify blind spots and growth opportunities.
