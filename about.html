The ECCSR model was developed by Joe Houghton to serve as a focus for project teams when engaging in any project. It is informed by many years of both practical and academic learnings in the project environment drawn from engagements in corporate, civil service and non-profit organizations.

When creating the various stages of a project, bearing the different aspects of this model in mind can serve to ensure that projects are aligned with current societal values and imperatives. Reviewing plans against the model can highlight areas of potential trouble if they have been overlooked, and also ensure that any project is capable of standing up to external scrutiny and oversight.

Ethical

Projects should be conducted with integrity, transparency and respect for all stakeholders. Ethical considerations should guide decision-making at every stage.

Collaborative

Effective teamwork and stakeholder engagement are essential. Collaboration fosters innovation and ensures diverse perspectives are considered.

Creating

Projects should generate tangible value and innovative solutions. The focus is on creating meaningful outcomes that benefit all parties.

Sustainable

Solutions should be environmentally responsible and economically viable long-term. Sustainability ensures projects deliver lasting benefits.

Results-oriented

Clear objectives and measurable outcomes are crucial. Projects must deliver concrete results that align with strategic goals.
