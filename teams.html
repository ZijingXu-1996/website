In project management, the team is the core force that drives project success. An efficient team needs to clarify the division of roles, establish a clear communication mechanism, and adopt appropriate collaboration tools to improve efficiency. For small businesses and charities, the resources we can use are limited, so it is even more necessary to optimize team collaboration.

It is recommended to formulate feasible team rules and regulations, which requires us to clarify common goals and codes of conduct, organize regular feedback meetings, and ensure that team members stay in sync. The collaboration principle in the ECCSR framework emphasizes knowledge sharing and mutual support, which is very important for small businesses and charities. For example, a social enterprise in Dublin has increased project delivery efficiency by 30% through weekly virtual meetings and shared task boards, and this initiative has also enhanced team cohesion.
