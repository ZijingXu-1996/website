Stakeholders in the ECCSR Framework

Effective stakeholder management is the guarantee for the smooth progress of the project. For small enterprises, this means identifying all parties affected by the project. This guide recommends drawing a power/interest matrix, understanding their needs and concerns, and communicating with them appropriately to identify key personnel and customize communication strategies to ensure that project outcomes are ethical, collaborative, and sustainable.

Stakeholder Engagement Strategies

Tailored Communication Approaches

  • High Influence/High Interest: Face-to-face meetings, personal reports
  • High Influence/Low Interest: Executive summaries, briefings
  • Low Influence/High Interest: Newsletters, community forums
  • Low Influence/Low Interest: General announcements, website updates

Levels of Participation

  • Inform: One-way information sharing
  • Consult: Gather input through surveys/interviews
  • Involve: Work directly with stakeholders
  • Collaborate: Partner in decision-making
  • Empower: Place final decision-making in stakeholders' hands

Effective Feedback Mechanisms

  • Regular satisfaction surveys
  • Dedicated feedback channels (email, phone)
  • Complaint resolution processes
  • Public response to concerns
  • Demonstration of how feedback influenced decisions

Case Study: Cork Housing Development

A property developer improved project outcomes through stakeholder management:

  • Early engagement with local residents and council
  • Monthly community update meetings
  • Dedicated liaison officer for stakeholder queries
  • Incorporated community feedback into design changes

This approach reduced objections by 70% and accelerated planning permission by 3 months.
