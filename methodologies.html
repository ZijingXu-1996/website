Methodologies in the ECCSR Framework

Choosing the right project management methodology should be based on project characteristics and organizational needs, which is especially important for small organizations. For small-scale projects, innovative projects with changing requirements are suitable for Scrum, while the guide recommends that traditional projects with fixed processes can adopt the waterfall model, while design sprints are suitable for rapid prototyping.

Find Your Methodology

Agile

Iterative approach that welcomes changing requirements, delivering working software frequently.

  • Best for: Software development, innovative projects
  • ECCSR Alignment: High on Collaboration, adapts to Ethical considerations
Learn More

Waterfall

Linear, sequential approach where progress flows downward through phases.

  • Best for: Construction, manufacturing, projects with fixed requirements
  • ECCSR Alignment: Strong on Results, clear Sustainability planning
Learn More

Scrum

Lightweight Agile framework with fixed-length iterations called sprints.

  • Best for: Product development, marketing campaigns
  • ECCSR Alignment: Excellent for team Collaboration, measurable Results
Learn More

Kanban

Visual workflow management method that limits work in progress.

  • Best for: Maintenance, support, continuous delivery
  • ECCSR Alignment: Promotes sustainable pace (Sustainability)
Learn More

PRINCE2

Process-based method for effective project management with defined roles.

  • Best for: Large-scale projects, government work
  • ECCSR Alignment: Strong Governance, clear Ethical guidelines
Learn More

Lean

Focuses on delivering value while eliminating waste in processes.

  • Best for: Startups, process improvement
  • ECCSR Alignment: Sustainable practices, Ethical resource use
Learn More

Hybrid Approaches

Many Irish small businesses combine methodologies to suit their specific needs. Here are some common hybrid approaches:

Scrumban

Combines Scrum's structure with Kanban's flexibility. Uses sprints but visualizes workflow on a Kanban board.

Scrum + Kanban

Agile-Waterfall

Waterfall for high-level planning with Agile execution phases. Common in construction projects.

Waterfall + Agile

Lean Startup

Applies Lean principles to startup environments with rapid prototyping and customer feedback.

Lean + Agile
